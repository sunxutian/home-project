Saturday, June 20 — Day 2
Old City Icons — 🪪 Bring Passport
Morning
🕌 Mosque⛪ Cathedral
Hagia Sophia & Blue Mosque
圣索菲亚大教堂 & 蓝色清真寺
📍 Hagia Sophia Map 📍 Blue Mosque Map
Hagia Sophia — Free before 8am. €25 otherwise. Church → Mosque → Museum → Mosque. One of the most historically important buildings in the world.
Blue Mosque — Free entry. Wear long pants/skirt. The iconic blue tiles inside. 30 min is enough.
🍳 Breakfast: Seven Hills Restaurant (opens 8:30) — Incredible view of both Hagia Sophia and Blue Mosque. Feed seagulls!
🥯 Simit — Turkish sesame ring from street vendors. Great breakfast snack.
12:30 – 13:15
🍖 Lunch
Lunch: Şehzade Cağ Kebap
午餐 · Sirkeci 区
9-min walk downhill from Hagia Sophia. Smoky, melt-in-your-mouth tender wood-fired lamb skewers. Fits before 1:15 PM Klook meeting point at Chimney Bistro.
📍 Google Maps 🍖 TripAdvisor Reviews
Afternoon
🏛️ Palace€27.50
Topkapi Palace
托普卡帕宫 · 奥斯曼帝国旧皇宫
📍 Google Maps 🎟️ Klook Ticket Voucher
8–10 min walk from hotel. €27.50, children €4. Pre-book tickets — on-site queue 30–60 min. Don't try to see everything (1.5–2 hrs is enough).
🍦 Dondurma — Turkish stretchy ice cream near the palace. Watch the show!
Evening
💃 Show🍰 Dessert
Whirling Dervish Show & Dinner
旋转舞表演 · Kızlarağası Medresesi
5 min walk near Blue Mosque. Shows at 19:00 & 20:30. Can haggle price to ₺350–400. Courtyard with artisan shops and tea house.
📍 Google Maps
🍯 Hafız Mustafa 1864 (Sirkeci) — Famous for pistachio baklava. Turkey's most iconic sweet shop.
03
Sunday, June 21 — Day 3
Bosphorus & New City — 🪪 Bring Passport
Morning
🚢 Cruise
Bosphorus Strait Cruise
博斯普鲁斯海峡游船
📍 Google Maps (Eminönü Dock)
See the Europe-Asia divide from the water. The entire city skyline in one boat ride. Book 1–2 days ahead — morning/sunset slots fill fast.
🚊 Getting there: Tram 10 min or taxi 10–15 min from hotel (don't walk with kids — 20–25 min).
🐟 Balık Ekmek — Grilled fish sandwich from boats near the dock. Classic Istanbul street food.
12:00 – 13:30
🐟 Lunch
Lunch: Süper Mario Emin Usta
午餐 · Balık Dürüm 烤鱼卷 · Karaköy
9-min walk across the lower level of Galata Bridge. Incredible spiced boneless fish wrap drizzled with pomegranate molasses.
📍 Google Maps 🐟 TripAdvisor Reviews
14:00 – 16:30
🏛️ Palace₺2000
Dolmabahçe Palace
多尔玛巴赫切宫 · 新皇宫 · 海边
📍 Google Maps
Ottoman "new palace" on the Bosphorus. T1 tram 1 stop from Karaköy.
⚠️ Sunday is the busiest day! Pre-book skip-the-line tickets. Without them, queue = 45–60+ min.
17:00 – 18:00
🚋 Street🛍️ Shopping
Istiklal Street & Taksim Square
独立大街 & 塔克西姆广场 · 坐复古电车
📍 Taksim Square 📍 Istiklal Street
F1 Funicular up to Taksim → walk down Istiklal (1.4km). Like NYC's Times Square + shopping street. Ride the nostalgic red tram!
🍦 Mado ice cream on Istiklal Street + Islak Burger (wet burger) at Taksim Square.
18:15 – 19:15
🗼 Tower₺130
Galata Tower
加拉塔塔 · 360° 城市全景
📍 Google Maps
10 min walk downhill from Istiklal. Elevator to observation deck. Queue ~30–45 min on Sundays. Sunset at ~20:40 if you can stay!
~19:30+
🍷 Dinner
Dinner in New City
新城区晚餐 · 氛围更好
🥮 Karaköy Güllüoğlu — Century-old baklava shop in Karaköy. A must-try!
📍 Google Maps 🥮 TripAdvisor Reviews

Monday, June 22 — Day 4
Bazaars & Markets
Morning
🛍️ Market
Grand Bazaar
大巴扎 · 世界最大室内市场之一
📍 Google Maps
12–15 min walk from hotel. Lamps, carpets, ceramics, souvenirs. Explore but keep an eye on time!
🧃 Fresh Pomegranate Juice — Squeezed fresh at the bazaar. + Midye Dolma (stuffed mussels).
11:30 – 14:00
🥙 Lunch
Lunch: Gaziantepli Çavuşoğlu
午餐 · Lahmacun 土耳其披萨
Inside Grand Bazaar area, near market gates. Ultra-thin, blistering-hot Lahmacun. Roll it up with lemon and parsley. Skip the sit-down tourist cafés.
📍 Google Maps 🥙 TripAdvisor Reviews
Afternoon
🧂 Spices☕ Coffee
Spice Bazaar & Snacks
香料市场 / 埃及市场 · 比大巴扎小、好逛
T1 tram ~5 min or 15–20 min walk. Spices, nuts, Turkish delight. Smaller and more manageable than Grand Bazaar.
☕ Kurukahveci Mehmet Efendi — Turkey's most famous coffee brand since 1871. Near Spice Bazaar.
📍 Spice Bazaar Map ☕ Coffee Shop Map
14:30 – 16:30
🍬 Sweets
Ali Muhiddin Hacı Bekir
百年老店 · 正宗 Lokum 土耳其软糖
On the main street before the Spice Bazaar entrance. Birthplace of Turkish Delight since 1777. Grab pistachio or rosewater Lokum.
